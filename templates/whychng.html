The Status Quo

People take unnecessary antibiotic/ painkillers unhesitatingly, even though it's better to doubt or research how they actually affect them.

The Agendas That Create This Status Quo

The pharmaceutical companies do not have a moral perspective towards the issue, and are mostly driven by money.

Do we want to change this status quo?

We relate more to the people who take antibiotics unnecessarily, because they are the ones who are affected by this status quo and are not aware of it.

So Yes, we want change. People would become healthier; since a lot of diseases are caused by side-effects of antibiotics/ painkillers. Also some of them only help people because they believe so.

(Placebo Effect) A placebo is anything that seems to be a "real" medical treatment -- but isn't. It could be a pill, a shot, or some other type of "fake" treatment. What all placebos have in common is that they do not contain an active substance meant to affect health.

The Risks

Pharmaceutical companies would start a process of defending themselves in the interest of not losing money, it may include suing those who are trying to change this status quo or ruining their reputation.

Also, it may be hard for people who are used to take painkillers and antibiotics to stop taking them, since it may have become a routine.

Blind Spots

The dangers caused by taking too much antibiotics/ painkillers or unnecessary ones. We're brainwashed to believe everything a doctor tells us and we don't have a bigger vision.

What Do We Believe In?

We think that awareness should be raised continuously; people get used to ideas as a result of repetition, subsequently they'll gradually adapt to it.

We believe that antibiotics/ painkillers might be helpful when in need, but taking unnecessary ones or too much of them without understanding their effect is harmful and ignorant.
